# Jakoma / Repurly Monetisation Plan — Commercially Consistent Version

Updated: 30 April 2026

## Market-informed pricing decision

The pricing strategy should separate three markets rather than blending them:

1. **Cheap scheduling tools** are not the benchmark for Repurly. They set buyer expectations for basic posting, not for workflow control, approvals, lead tracking or supported operations.
2. **LinkedIn creator tools** validate a LinkedIn-specific wedge, but their lower price points are for individual creators and lighter personal-brand workflows.
3. **Team social workflow platforms and fractional leadership services** support a premium position where the value is operating rhythm, governance, approvals, reliability and commercial outcome.

Therefore the commercially consistent strategy is:

> Consulting funds the business. Repurly runs the marketing. Toolkits capture leads. Independent Living Tech creates local cashflow. Repurly is sold after internal proof, first as a supported pilot/service, then as SaaS.

## Why these prices are the right fit

### AI governance

The £950 triage is intentionally low-friction but not cheap. It gives a serious buyer a paid diagnostic without forcing a £3,500+ commitment immediately. The sprint remains the main consulting product at from £3,500, with a typical range of £3,500–£7,500 for SME and charity/housing/professional-services buyers. Retainers start at £2,000/month to create an accessible recurring entry point, but the active and strategic bands preserve room for senior-value pricing.

### Independent Living Tech

The £149 assessment is an easy local first sale. Setup pricing is separated from device costs to protect margin and avoid hardware risk. The service is positioned as practical technology setup, not care, clinical monitoring or emergency response.

### Toolkits

Toolkits are not the primary income engine. They are lead magnets and conversion bridges. £49, £149 and £299 creates a clean ladder from self-service to workshop, triage and consulting.

### Repurly

Repurly should not be priced at £79/month. That would make it look like another scheduler. The correct posture is premium workflow software with optional support:

- £950 founder pilot for early proof only
- £1,500+ guided pilot/onboarding as the normal paid setup route
- £297/month Core for focused teams
- £697/month Growth as the commercial default for multi-brand operators
- Custom Scale for higher-volume operations
