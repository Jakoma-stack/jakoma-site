Built on real leadership experience, not theory.

Jakoma is a founder-led consultancy shaped by more than 20 years of work across complex, high-accountability environments where data, governance, operational delivery and leadership decisions had to align.

That experience includes senior responsibility across data, analytics, information governance, digital delivery, risk and organisational decision-making. The result is a practical approach to AI and data consulting that understands both sides of the challenge: the need to move forward, and the need to do it in a way that stands up to scrutiny.

Jakoma was created for organisations that need more than advice. They need clear ownership, workable controls, usable documentation and delivery support that makes progress easier, not harder.

Most organisations do not fail because they lack ambition.

They struggle because delivery moves in one direction, governance tries to catch up later, and risk builds quietly in the gap.

Teams adopt tools before ownership is clear. Leaders want progress but do not yet have the right decision routes. Documentation is inconsistent. Supplier questions arrive late. Assurance becomes reactive. Good intentions turn into avoidable friction.

Jakoma closes that gap by helping organisations connect delivery, governance and accountability from the start.

Governance that helps people move.

Jakoma’s approach is designed for organisations that need practical progress in environments where trust, oversight and evidence matter.

Governance built into delivery

Roles, decision routes, minimum standards and evidence expectations are designed alongside delivery, not bolted on afterwards.

Outputs teams can actually use

Templates, registers, policy guidance, working practices and decision structures are created to be used by real teams, not admired in a folder.

Progress measured properly

The work is tied to practical outcomes, adoption signals and visible next steps so leaders can see what is improving and what still needs attention.

Why organisations choose this kind of support.

  • They need senior judgement without hiring a full-time post too early.
  • They need AI or automation work to move forward without governance drift.
  • They need proportionate controls instead of bureaucracy or guesswork.
  • They need clearer ownership, better decision routes and stronger accountability.
  • They need work that can stand up to audit, challenge or public scrutiny.
