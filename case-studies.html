Example Scenarios

What Jakoma work can look like in real situations.

These anonymised examples make the offer easier to understand without exposing client details. They show the kind of situations Jakoma is built to handle across organisations, homes, property and supported settings.

Proof without overclaiming

Example scenarios by route

These are not promises of specific outcomes. They are realistic examples of how a practical, accountable technology approach can translate into usable outputs.

Organisation

AI use has already started informally

Situation: Staff are using AI tools before the organisation has a clear register, policy route or assurance process.

Jakoma route: AI Governance Triage followed by a readiness sprint if needed.

Useful output: Prioritised risks, immediate controls, use-case register structure and a practical 30/60/90-day route.

Home and family

An older relative is becoming harder to reassure

Situation: Family members are worried about missed calls, night-time confidence, reminders or whether the person is safe at home.

Jakoma route: Short Home Tech enquiry, suitability check, then assessment or setup.

Useful output: Simple communication, routines, lighting, alerts or reassurance options without unnecessary gadgets.

Short-term rental

A host wants fewer check-in problems

Situation: Guests ask repeated questions about access, Wi-Fi, heating or house instructions.

Jakoma route: Smart Stay enquiry followed by Starter, Remote Host or Protection setup.

Useful output: Better guest instructions, access workflow, QR guide planning and owner handover.

Rental property

A landlord wants a safer, easier property

Situation: The property needs practical improvements around access, leak risk, lighting, Wi-Fi or support for a tenant living alone.

Jakoma route: Rental Property Tech Assessment or setup deposit once scope is clear.

Useful output: A manageable, rental-ready technology plan rather than an overcomplicated smart home.

Supported living

Technology exists, but workflows are unclear

Situation: Devices, alerts, Wi-Fi, cameras or family-access expectations are creating confusion for staff or residents.

Jakoma route: Supported Living Technology Review enquiry and scoped review.

Useful output: Practical review of usability, privacy, consent, workflows, escalation and next steps.

Products

A buyer wants a starting point before consultancy

Situation: The team wants structure, templates or a self-service starting point before committing to a larger project.

Jakoma route: Toolkits, checklists or productised packs.

Useful output: Practical prompts, templates and decision routes that support buyer confidence.

Use the examples to choose a route.

If one scenario feels close to your situation, open that service page and send the short enquiry form. If none fit, request a proposal and describe the problem.
