Privacy Notice

How Jakoma uses personal data across the website, enquiries, consultancy work, Independent Living Tech and productised services.

Last updated: 6 May 2026

This notice explains how Jakoma uses personal data. It is written for website visitors, prospective clients, customers, families making Independent Living enquiries, landlords, hosts, suppliers and professional contacts.

1. Who we are

Jakoma is the controller for personal data collected through this website, enquiry forms, email, calls and service delivery unless a written proposal or contract says otherwise. For privacy questions, contact support@jakoma.org.

Formal legal notices can be sent to support@jakoma.org unless a proposal, quote or contract gives a different address.

2. Personal data we collect

  • contact details, such as name, email address, phone number and organisation;
  • enquiry details, such as service interest, problem description, notes and attachments you choose to send;
  • business information needed for proposals, scoping, delivery, billing and support;
  • Independent Living Tech information, which may include household context, accessibility needs, support needs, device preferences and relevant property details;
  • website and analytics information, such as pages visited, referral source and consent preference, where analytics cookies are accepted;
  • communications records, such as emails, form submissions, call notes and project messages.

3. How we use personal data

  • to respond to enquiries and provide information about Jakoma services;
  • to prepare proposals, quotes, statements of work and service recommendations;
  • to deliver consultancy, analytics, automation, assurance, home technology or property technology services;
  • to manage customer relationships, scheduling, billing, records and support;
  • to improve the website, content, services and conversion journeys;
  • to meet legal, tax, accounting, security and regulatory obligations;
  • to send service-related communications and, where permitted, relevant marketing updates.

4. Lawful bases

  • Contract: to take steps before entering into a contract and to deliver agreed services.
  • Legitimate interests: to respond to business enquiries, improve services, maintain records, protect the website and run proportionate B2B relationship management.
  • Consent: for optional analytics cookies and any consent-based marketing or special-category information you choose to provide where consent is the appropriate basis.
  • Legal obligation: for records needed for tax, accounting, legal claims or regulatory requirements.

5. Sensitive or special-category information

Independent Living Tech enquiries may involve information about health, disability, support needs or living arrangements. Only share what is necessary for Jakoma to understand the request. Jakoma uses this information to assess needs, suggest proportionate technology options and deliver the requested service. Where appropriate, Jakoma will ask for explicit consent or rely on another lawful condition under UK data protection law.

6. Who we share data with

Jakoma may share data with trusted service providers that support hosting, forms, email, analytics, customer management, payment, accounting, scheduling, document storage and service delivery. Jakoma may also share data with professional advisers, insurers, regulators, law enforcement or courts where required. For home technology work, information may be shared with device suppliers, installers or support contacts where necessary and appropriate.

7. International transfers

Some suppliers may process data outside the UK. Where this happens, Jakoma will rely on appropriate safeguards such as adequacy regulations, standard contractual clauses or supplier terms designed to protect personal data.

8. Retention

  • Website analytics and consent records: retained according to the settings of the relevant analytics and consent tools.
  • General enquiries: normally retained for up to 24 months unless they become customer records or need to be kept longer.
  • Customer, proposal, project and billing records: normally retained for up to 7 years for tax, accounting, contractual and legal reasons.
  • Independent Living Tech notes: retained only for as long as needed for support, safety, warranty, record keeping or legal reasons.

9. Your rights

You may have rights to access, correct, erase, restrict, object to use of your data, withdraw consent and request portability. You can also complain to the Information Commissioner’s Office. Contact Jakoma first at support@jakoma.org so the issue can be reviewed promptly.

10. Marketing

Jakoma may send relevant business communications where permitted by law. You can opt out at any time. Jakoma does not sell personal data to advertisers.

11. Cookies and analytics

See the Cookie Policy for details of essential cookies, optional analytics cookies and how to change your choice.

12. Updates

This notice may be updated as Jakoma’s services, suppliers or legal obligations change.
